My name is Kent Anderson and I\u2019m an editor at Sports Raid, a Medium page. As it is the beginning of 2023, I thought it would be a good idea to put out a clarion call for new writers and submissions.<\/p>\n

I have been a working journalist for 40-plus years. I\u2019ve covered both college and pro sports and worked at both the Detroit News and Free Press, in addition to the Observer&Eccentric newspaper chain during my time in the business. I have also been an assistant sports editor and sports editor at Wayne State University and Schoolcraft College. I have also been an editorial page columnist and writer for the defunct disability website icanonline.net.<\/p>\n

While sports isn\u2019t a huge topic here, it does play a huge role in society and impacts our lives more and more. From public funding of stadiums and arenas, to the proliferation of gambling in nearly every state and the sorry stories of harassment and ownership more interested in the art of appearances rather than concrete solutions, sports play an important role in our everyday lives.<\/p>\n

I follow the AP stylebook, as well as common sense and logic-based writing and facts. Attributions and links to sources are also required. Facts matter as does the truth. Also, if you use \u2018we,\u2019 \u2018I,\u2019 or \u2018our,\u2019 in a piece, your article will be edited.<\/p>\n

The best sports columnists I\u2019ve ever read, Jerry Green of The Detroit News, Mike Downey of the Detroit Free Press, Los Angeles Times and Chicago Tribune and Dave Anderson of The New York Times, hardly ever injected themselves into their stories.<\/p>\n

You immerse the reader into a narrative and tell a story. That\u2019s what grabs a reader, not the writer\u2019s experience. Journalism, especially sports journalism, is about people who make the news, not those reporting it. It seems to have turned into that recently.<\/p>\n

In the meantime, there\u2019s plenty to write about these days. Whether it\u2019s the hypocrisy of the NCAA, the corruption of FIFA and the IOC, the NFL\u2019s battle over turf surfaces, LIV golf and the PGA, the rise of women\u2019s sports, MLB\u2019s continuing quest to stay relevant in a changing society and the brilliance of Connor McDavid (or as an old hockey writer friend calls him \u2018Connor McJesus\u2019).<\/p>\n

That\u2019s a lot of material to cover. So, send your submissions and join me in providing insight and analysis into sport and the business of sports.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
