In this article, I use the newest updated public dataset taken from\u00a0the\u00a0<\/strong>sofifa.com<\/strong><\/a>\u00a0website<\/strong>\u00a0that contains detailed information\u00a0about 17,600 football players from FIFA23 including their weekly salaries.\u00a0<\/strong>The dataset is also\u00a0publicly available on Kaggle<\/strong><\/a>. Full details of the analysis can be found\u00a0in this public Kaggle notebook<\/strong><\/a>.<\/p>\n

Step 1 \u2014 data preprocessing<\/h1>\n

Here, data preprocessing consists of the following steps:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • converting the label (weekly salaries<\/strong>) to\u00a0kEUR\/week<\/strong>;<\/li>\n
  • selecting only\u00a0players with non-zero (positive) salaries, and log10-transforming the labels<\/strong>;<\/li>\n
  • excluding\u00a01% of the highest and 1% of the smallest salaries<\/strong>\u00a0as potential outliers;<\/li>\n
  • extracting full years from date columns;<\/li>\n
  • selecting players only with valid contracts;<\/li>\n
  • transforming\u00a0International Reputation<\/strong>,\u00a0Weak Foot<\/strong>, and\u00a0Skill Moves<\/strong>\u00a0columns (measured in stars, from 1 to 5<\/strong>) to strings;<\/li>\n
  • grouping\u00a0Overall<\/strong>,\u00a0Potential<\/strong>, and\u00a0Special<\/strong>\u00a0columns to larger bins;<\/li>\n
  • encoding\u00a0rare categorical variables<\/a>\u00a0(in\u00a0Nationality<\/strong>,\u00a0Preferred Foot<\/strong>, and\u00a0Body Type<\/strong>\u00a0columns) with no more than\u00a070<\/strong>\u00a0different categories in each column and at least\u00a030<\/strong>\u00a0data samples in each category;<\/li>\n
  • finally, dropping unused columns.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    As a result, the cleaned dataset contains\u00a0about 10,000 players salaries<\/strong>,\u00a0ranging from 550 to 90,000 EUR\/week<\/strong>.<\/p>\n

    Step 2 \u2014 setting a Machine Learning model to predict the weekly salaries<\/h1>\n

    The data prepared with the previous step are randomly split between training and test samples and modelled with the\u00a0CatBoostRegressor<\/a>\u00a0model that explicitly takes into account categorical features. The\u00a0root mean squared error<\/a>\u00a0(RMSE) of the resulting model is\u00a0about 0.279\u00a0<\/strong>dex<\/strong><\/a>\u00a0points<\/strong>, a significant\u00a0improvement compared to the baseline model RMSE of about 0.583 dex\u00a0<\/strong>(assuming the same\u00a0salary of about 3,700 EUR\/week\u00a0<\/strong>for\u00a0every<\/strong>\u00a0player).<\/p>\n

    Step 3 \u2014 explanation of the obtained Machine Learning model.<\/h1>\n

    Here, we are using the\u00a0SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP)<\/a>\u00a0method, one of the most common to explore the explainability of Machine Learning models. The units of SHAP value are hence in\u00a0dex<\/strong><\/a>\u00a0points<\/strong>.<\/p>\n
